China's economy

  After the founding of the People's Republic of China, through large-scale construction in a planned way, China has become one of the largest economies in the world with the greatest potential for development. According to the plan, China will establish a relatively perfect market economy system by 2010； We will establish a mature market economy by 2020。

  From 1953 to 2010, China has completed 11 "five-year plans" and established a mature market economy system by 2020 It has made remarkable achievements and laid a solid foundation for the development of the national economy. The reform and opening up since 1979 has led to unprecedented rapid growth of China's economy. After entering the 21st century, China's economy has maintained steady and rapid growth. The market economic system has been preliminarily established, and the market plays a decisive role in the allocation of resources. The macro-control system is improving day by day. Take public ownership economy as main body, individual, private, foreign capital The pattern of common development of the non-public sector of the economy is basically formed. The pattern of economic growth is gradually changing from extensive to intensive.

Five to 10 years from now,
  The trend and policy orientation of China's economic development

  First, adjust the economic structure. This is the main line of economic development for a long time to come. We need to adjust the economic structure to take China's economic development to a new level. And not just a low expansion of total GDP. Industrial structure adjustment is the main content of current economic structure adjustment . The imbalance of technological and economic development in China requires attention to deal with two relations in the adjustment of industrial structure: One is the relationship between the development of emerging industries and the transformation of traditional industries with new technologies. Second, the relationship between developing capital and technology intensive industries and developing labor intensive industries.

  Second, accelerate urbanization. Rural economic development has long - term problems. Since the 1990s, there have been two basic situations: first, the average annual growth rate of grain output is significantly higher than that of population. Second, the per capita grain ration consumption of urban and rural residents declined. This has led to a considerable number of difficulties in the sale of agricultural products that have not been seen in the past. Many agricultural products are higher than the international market price, it is difficult to raise agricultural prices to increase farmers' income. China's agricultural yield per unit area is not low, and the root cause of not making money in growing grain is that there are more people and less land It is difficult for peasants to become rich if they do not manage on a large scale.

  Third, western development. The imbalance of regional economic development is a universal law. In China's thousands of years of history, regional development has been unbalanced. In both the east and the west, between cities and counties within provinces and autonomous regions, The gap is widening, which is a problem to be solved in local economic development . Regional economic policy should emphasize two big picture. It is a big picture to allow some areas to get rich first, and this policy should be adhered to.

  Fourth, achieve sustainable development. This is a worldwide issue. In the new stage of development, sustainable development should be highlighted. Mainly population, environment.

  Fifth, improve the market economy. It is only "preliminary" to a market economy, that is, With a prototype, a big framework, there are many imperfections, We still need to emphasize institutional innovation and provide institutional guarantee for economic development through deepening reform. China's accession to the WTO marks that China's economic reform has entered a new stage of development. It is the stage of establishing a relatively perfect socialist market economy system that is in line with the rules of international market economy. Institutional innovation is an indispensable part of China's modernization drive

  Sixth, develop an open economy. China's international economic environment and foreign economic relations have undergone profound changes. This is mainly due to economic globalization and China's opening-up. Economic globalization is a trend. Interwoven with the complex interests of various countries and fierce competition, it is inevitable to go through many twists and turns. The consequences can be both positive and negative

  Seventh, improve people's living standards. We should properly handle the relationship between national construction and people's lives, and between accumulation and consumption We should give consideration to both national interests and people's livelihood. In social policy, we should pay attention to adjusting social distribution and solving the problem of employment. China faces heavy employment pressure. 40 million new urban jobs will be created in the next five years. The agricultural labor force transferred 40 million to non-agricultural industries
